Publications

  • Ahamed, T., Zou, B., Farazi, N.P., & Tulabandhula, T. (2021). Deep reinforcement learning for crowdsourced urban delivery. Transportation Research Part B: Methodological, 152, 227-257. Download PDF

    Synopsis

    • Investigated assigning shipping requests to crowdsourcees with time and capacity constraints.
    • Proposed a centralized, deep reinforcement learning-based approach.
    • Presented a new state space representation encompassing spatial-temporal and capacity information.
    • Embedded heuristics-guided action choice in DRL to preserve tractability and enhance efficiency.
    • Integrated rule-interposing into DRL to further enhance training and implementation efficiency.

  • Farazi, N.P., Zou, B., Ahamed, T., & Barua, L. (2021). Deep reinforcement learning in transportation research: A review. Transportation Research Interdisciplinary Perspectives, 11, 100425. Download PDF

    Synopsis

    • Reviewed the development and applications of DRL in transportation research.
    • Identified seven domains where DRL has been applied and adapted in transportation.
    • Discussed the applicability, strengths, and shortcomings of DRL in transportation research.
    • Identified common and application-specific issues and suggested future research directions.
    • Provided information on available platforms and features for DRL implementation.

  • Ahamed, T. and Zou, B., 2021. Multi-tier Adaptive Memory Programming and Cluster- and Job-based Relocation for Distributed On-demand Crowdshipping. arXiv preprint arXiv:2105.13845. Download PDF

    Synopsis

    • Propose a novel framework for distributed on-demand crowdshipping that leverages multi-tier adaptive memory programming and cluster- and job-based relocation.
    • Address several key issues in crowdshipping, including user incentives, matching, and routing.
    • Conduct simulation experiments to evaluate the performance of the proposed framework in comparison to other state-of-the-art methods.
    • Demonstrate that the proposed framework achieves significant improvements in terms of delivery time, cost, and user satisfaction.

  • Ahamed, T., 2022. Towards Urban Delivery Innovations: Macro- and Micro-Level Models of Crowdshipping (Doctoral dissertation, University of Illinois at Chicago). Download PDF

    Synopsis

    • Develop macro- and micro-level models of crowdshipping as a means of addressing urban delivery challenges.
    • Investigate various factors that affect the performance of crowdshipping systems, such as spatial and temporal dynamics, user behavior, and market characteristics.
    • Propose optimization models and algorithms for improving the efficiency and effectiveness of crowdshipping systems, and evaluate their performance using real-world data.
    • Provide insights into the design and implementation of crowdshipping systems, including platform features, user interfaces, and pricing strategies.
    • Identify future research directions and opportunities for advancing the field of crowdshipping.
